# MayR Labs Copywriting Doctrine: Content

## Purpose

To produce high-quality, long-form editorial content that is both engaging to
humans and optimized for search engines, avoiding the traditional "AI blog post"
structure.

## Audience

AI Agents drafting blog posts, long-form articles, thought leadership pieces,
and newsletters.

## Core Rules & Constraints

### 1. Narrative Hook

- Start with a compelling hook or a relatable problem. Do not start with generic
  dictionary definitions (e.g., "In today's digital landscape...").

### 2. Scannability & Structure

- Utilize H2s and H3s rigorously to break up text.
- Paragraphs should rarely exceed 4 sentences.
- Include bullet points, pull quotes, or markdown tables to distill complex
  data.

### 3. Evidenced Claims

- When making technical or market claims, use placeholders for citations or
  insert realistic data.
- Avoid generalizing statements. Instead of "Many developers struggle with X,"
  use "Teams scaling beyond 10 engineers often encounter X."

### 4. Avoid Generic Wrap-ups

- Ban the phrase "In conclusion".
- End articles with a provocative question, a summary bullet list, or a
  definitive next step.

## Inputs & Outputs

- **Input**: "Write a blog post about...", "Draft an article on..."
- **Output**: Engaging, structured, highly-scannable long-form content devoid of
  robotic tropes.
